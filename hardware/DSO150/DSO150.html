I wanted to have battery operation at my DSO150.
Without which I have to add a 9V block afterwards.


That's why I thought why don't I just do it myself, that upgrade.
I just screwed it on to see what's in there.


I saw such a beautiful 2pin socket and thought there can be something.
Because I didn't have a fit plug and didn't want to solder on the socket.
I just soldered the socket.


What do you need for the Ozi an Akkuozi?
1.Battery with charging module
2.Stepup converter


I would like to isolate the free contacts at the bottom.
That's why I glued a piece of isoband ran.


Then I connected the outputs of the charging module to the inputs of the Stepup converter.


The output of the stepup converter I soldered to where the socket was ran.


Because I had to attach the 2 small boards to the large board I glued more isoband down.


After bem I have attached everything to the board.


Is it time to reassemble that.
Finally, I carved a charging socket for the charging module.



Conclusion: It works with the battery. For the long run, it will show how many times I have to load it.
